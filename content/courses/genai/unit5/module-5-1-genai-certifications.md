## What Indian employers actually look for

Before listing certifications, the honest context: a hiring manager at Goldman Sachs India put it directly — "When I see 8 EdTech badges and zero vendor certifications, I actually view it negatively — it suggests the candidate chose easy wins over meaningful credentials."

<div class="mod-keypoint">
<strong>The rule of thumb for Indian IT in 2026:</strong> One strong vendor certification (Google, Microsoft, AWS) paired with a real portfolio project outperforms five EdTech badges every time. Certifications signal that you studied. Projects prove that you can build. The strongest profiles have both — in that priority order: project first, certification second.
</div>

## The financial aid route — getting paid certificates free

All Coursera certifications offer financial aid. Most Indian students do not know how easy the application is.

**Process:** Open any Coursera course → Click "Enroll for Free" → Scroll down → Click "Financial Aid available" → Fill a 200-word application explaining your situation → Submit. Approval rate is approximately 80% within 15 days. The application is evaluated honestly — if you genuinely cannot afford the certificate, you will likely be approved.

**What to write:** Be specific and honest. "I am a final-year BTech student in [city]. The course certificate will help me in campus placements at companies like TCS and Infosys. I cannot currently afford the fee because [reason]." 200 words is enough.

This route gives you the same certificate as anyone who paid full price. No marking of financial aid on the certificate — it looks identical.
